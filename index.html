UNITAS Program Highlights (Guided by iTokyo Academy)

UNITAS Japanese Language School offers a variety of courses to suit different learning goals. iTokyo Academy will help you identify and apply for the program that best aligns with your aspirations.

General Japanese Course

Develop comprehensive Japanese skills (reading, writing, listening, speaking) for daily life, further education, or career advancement. Suitable for all levels from beginner to advanced.

University Preparatory Course

Focused preparation for EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students) and support for applying to Japanese universities or vocational schools.

Business Japanese Course

Learn Japanese specific to professional environments, including business etiquette, keigo (honorific language), and communication skills for the Japanese workplace.

Short-Term Courses

Ideal for those on a tourist visa or with limited time. Experience Japanese language and culture through intensive study programs ranging from a few weeks to three months.

The iTokyo Academy Gold-Standard Document Checklist

Perfection in documentation is non-negotiable. This exhaustive checklist, developed by iTokyo Academy's seasoned experts, outlines all 16 critical document sets. Your iTokyo Academy consultant will provide personalized templates and scrutinize each item for compliance before submission.

iTokyo Academy's Unwavering Submission Protocol:

  • Maintain the integrity of the SINGLE EMAIL THREAD for all document exchanges with iTokyo Academy.
  • Adhere to iTokyo Academy's PRESCRIBED FILENAMING CONVENTIONS for impeccable organization.

Submit All Final, iTokyo Academy-Verified Documents To: unitasadmission@itokyoacademy.com

Ensure CC To Your iTokyo Academy Primary Contact: kumar@itokyoacademy.com

  1. Application Forms (Excel – Form 1, 2, & 3: iTokyo Academy Version)

    iTokyo Academy provides the official, latest versions of these forms and offers line-by-line guidance to ensure error-free completion.

    • Fill in BLOCK CAPITAL LETTERS ONLY, as per Japanese immigration standards.
    • Your Full Name: "SURNAME FIRSTNAME MIDDLENAME" (precisely as it appears on your passport). Example: Surname: Ganesan, Given Name: Vinothini → GANESAN VINOTHINI.
    • Address: Use the permanent address from Your Passport. Any deviation must be explained and documented, as advised by iTokyo Academy.
    • Course Applied for: "General Japanese Language Course" (or specific course advised by iTokyo Academy).
    • Accommodation Preference: "To be decided" (UNITAS or iTokyo Academy can assist with options later).
    • Future Plans (in form): Clearly articulate your post-study goals (e.g., "Seek employment in IT sector in Japan leveraging Japanese skills"). iTokyo Academy helps refine this statement.
    • Sponsor's Full Legal Name: Must PERFECTLY MATCH all financial and identification documents (ITR, Bank Records, Passport/Aadhaar).
    • Form 2 (Detailed Education History): List all formal education from 1st Standard (Primary School) onwards. EXCLUDE Kindergarten/Nursery. Ensure no gaps in timelines.
    • School/College Names: Use FULL OFFICIAL NAMES (e.g., "Kendriya Vidyalaya No. 1" ✅ not "KV No. 1" ❌).
    • Graduation Dates: Mention exact Month & Year as per official certificates/transcripts.
    • Reason For Study (in form): A concise, impactful summary (approx. 5-7 lines). iTokyo Academy provides examples and review.
  2. Statement of Purpose (Reason for Study - PDF/Word)

    A compelling narrative (approx. 2-3 well-structured paragraphs, ~300-400 words). Explain your motivations: Why Japanese? Why Japan? Why UNITAS? How does this align with your past experiences and future aspirations? iTokyo Academy offers critical feedback to enhance its persuasiveness.

  3. Future Path & Career Goals Explanation (PDF/Word)

    A detailed plan (approx. 2-3 paragraphs, ~300 words). Articulate your specific career objectives post-UNITAS and how the Japanese language proficiency gained will be instrumental. Mention target industries or roles if possible. iTokyo Academy helps you craft a realistic and convincing statement.

  4. Academic Transcripts & Degree Certificate(s)

    Clear, notarized (if required by embassy/UNITAS, iTokyo Academy will advise) color scans of all marksheets and the final degree/diploma certificate for your highest completed qualification (and 12th/10th). If currently studying, provide latest marksheets and a Bonafide Certificate.

  5. JLPT/NAT/Other Japanese Proficiency Certificate

    Official certificate of any Japanese language proficiency test passed (e.g., JLPT N5, N4, NAT Q5, Q4). This is a highly valued asset. If results are pending, provide the admit card/registration proof. iTokyo Academy emphasizes the importance of this.

  6. Japanese Language Study Hours Certificate (from Institute)

    An official certificate from your Japanese language training institute detailing the total number of study hours completed (e.g., 150 hours, 300 hours) and the levels/textbooks covered. iTokyo Academy can provide a sample format if needed.

  7. Passport Copy (All Pages with Markings)

    High-resolution, color scans of ALL pages of your current passport, including blank ones. Ensure passport is valid for at least 1 year from your intended start date in Japan. Previous passports with travel history (especially to Japan) should also be included if advised by iTokyo Academy.

  8. Sponsor-Applicant Relationship Proof (Official Documents)

    Legally valid documents clearly establishing the relationship:

    • Parents as sponsor: Your Birth Certificate, Sponsor's Passport/Aadhaar.
    • Other relatives: A detailed Family Tree Chart (genealogy) supported by relevant birth certificates/official records, and an Affidavit of Support. iTokyo Academy provides templates and guidance for complex cases.
  9. Sponsor's 1-Year Bank Statement (Original/Attested)

    Original or bank-attested statements for the past 12 consecutive months for the primary account showing consistent financial capacity and the required balance. Must clearly show account holder's name, account number, and bank's stamp/seal on every page. iTokyo Academy helps analyze this for red flags.

  10. Explanation Letter for Large/Recent Deposits (If Applicable)

    Mandatory if any significant deposits (e.g., >2-3 Lakhs INR, or pattern of deposits leading to the required balance) occurred in the sponsor's account, especially in the last 3-6 months. Letter must detail the source of funds with irrefutable documentary evidence (e.g., sale deed, PF withdrawal statement, gift deed from close relative with their financials). iTokyo Academy offers expert assistance in structuring this critical letter.

  11. Sponsor's Bank Balance Certificate (Original, Recent)

    An original certificate from the sponsor's bank, issued recently (within 1 month of submission), confirming a closing balance of at least 10-12 Lakhs INR (or JPY equivalent as advised by iTokyo Academy). Must be on bank letterhead, signed, and stamped.

  12. Sponsor's Income Tax Returns (Last 3 Years) & PAN Card

    Complete ITR documents (including computation of income, acknowledgement) for the sponsor for the last three assessment years. Also, a clear copy of the sponsor's PAN card. This demonstrates consistent income. iTokyo Academy verifies completeness.

  13. Applicant's Name Discrepancy Affidavit (If Applicable)

    If your name (or parents' names) varies across academic certificates, passport, Aadhaar, etc., a notarized affidavit explaining the variations and confirming the correct legal name is required. iTokyo Academy provides standard formats.

  14. Sponsor's Name Discrepancy Affidavit (If Applicable)

    Similarly, if the sponsor's name has variations across their official documents (ITR, bank, ID proofs), a notarized affidavit is needed. iTokyo Academy ensures this is correctly prepared.

  15. Applicant's Work Experience Proof (If Any Gap or Relevant)

    If there's a gap between your last education and application, or if your work experience is relevant to your study plan: Appointment letters, experience certificates, salary slips (last 3 months), relieving letters. iTokyo Academy advises on how to best present this to explain study gaps.

  16. Applicant's Passport-Size Photographs (Specifics by iTokyo Academy)

    Recent (within last 3 months), high-quality, professional photographs.

    • Quantity: 1 Digital Soft Copy (JPEG, high-res), 4-6 Physical Hard Copies.
    • Specifications: 3cm x 4cm or 3.5cm x 4.5cm (as per latest UNITAS/Embassy guidelines - iTokyo Academy will confirm), plain white background, matte finish, 80% face coverage, no spectacles/headgear (unless religious and clearly shows face).

As a student visa holder in Japan, you can apply for a work permit ("Permission to Engage in Activity Other Than That Permitted by the Status of Residence Previously Granted"). This allows you to work:

  • Up to 28 hours per week during school terms.
  • Up to 40 hours per week (8 hours a day) during long school vacations.

Common part-time jobs include roles in restaurants, convenience stores, teaching your native language, etc. iTokyo Academy and UNITAS provide general guidance on job searching and understanding work regulations. Your Japanese proficiency will significantly impact job opportunities.

UNITAS generally has four main intake periods for long-term courses:

  • April Term: Application deadline usually by end of October of the previous year.
  • July Term: Application deadline usually by end of February.
  • October Term: Application deadline usually by end of April.
  • January Term: Application deadline usually by end of August of the previous year.

iTokyo Academy strongly advises starting your application process well in advance of these deadlines. Contact us for the most current and specific deadline information for your desired intake.
